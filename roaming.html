what is roaming?

Roaming is a service offered by mobile network operators (MNOs) that allows their customers to use their mobile devices, such as smartphones or tablets, while traveling outside of their home network coverage area. When a mobile device user travels to an area outside of their home network coverage, the device will automatically search for and connect to a compatible network within the roaming partner network of their MNO. This enables the device user to continue to use their device to make and receive calls, send and receive text messages, and access the Internet, as if they were still in their home network coverage area. The MNO typically charges the user for the use of the network services while roaming, often at higher rates than those charged for home network usage, due to the costs associated with providing access to the partner network. Roaming is particularly useful for travelers who need to stay connected to the Internet and communicate with others while traveling outside of their home network coverage area. However, users should be aware of the potential for higher charges and may wish to check with their MNO to understand the specific rates and charges associated with roaming before using their devices while traveling.
