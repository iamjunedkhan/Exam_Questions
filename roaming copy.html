How silly window syndrome is related to error during transmission?

The Silly Window Syndrome is a networking issue that can occur when sending data over a network connection. It happens when a receiving device gets a small amount of data (less than its buffer size) and must wait for more data to arrive before it can process the information.

During this waiting time, the receiving device may send an acknowledgement back to the sender, indicating that it's ready for more data. However, if the sender sends only a small amount of data again, the receiver's buffer may become unnecessarily full, resulting in a delay in processing the information and potentially causing errors during transmission.

This delay can cause issues such as reduced throughput, increased latency, and timeouts, which can lead to transmission errors such as dropped packets or corrupted data. Therefore, the Silly Window Syndrome is closely related to errors during transmission, as it can lead to a variety of issues that can result in transmission errors.
